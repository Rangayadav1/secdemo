About MS Dhoni

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, popularly known as MS Dhoni, is a former captain of the Indian cricket team. Born on July 7, 1981, in Ranchi, India, he has made a monumental impact on international cricket. Known for his cool demeanor, aggressive captaincy, and finishing skills, MS Dhoni led India to victory in the ICC World T20 2007, ICC World Cup 2011, and ICC Champions Trophy 2013.

Dhoni is regarded as one of the best wicket-keepers and finishers in limited-overs cricket. He holds numerous records in international cricket and continues to inspire millions of fans worldwide.

Major Achievements

ICC World Cup 2011

MS Dhoni captained India to its second ICC World Cup victory in 2011 after a gap of 28 years. He played a match-winning knock of 91* in the final against Sri Lanka.

ICC T20 World Cup 2007

In his first major ICC tournament as captain, Dhoni led India to victory in the inaugural ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, defeating Pakistan in the final.

ICC Champions Trophy 2013

Dhoni became the first captain to win all three major ICC trophies (World Cup, T20 World Cup, and Champions Trophy) when he led India to victory in the ICC Champions Trophy 2013 in England.

Indian Premier League (IPL)

Dhoni has been the captain of the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) since the inception of IPL. Under his leadership, CSK won 3 IPL titles (2010, 2011, and 2018).
