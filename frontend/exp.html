Professional Summary

Mahendra Singh Dhoni, former captain of the Indian cricket team, is known for his calm leadership, exceptional wicket-keeping skills, and match-winning batting performances. Under his captaincy, India won the ICC T20 World Cup (2007), ICC ODI World Cup (2011), and ICC Champions Trophy (2013). Beyond cricket, Dhoni is an entrepreneur and philanthropist with a passion for mentoring young athletes.

Major Achievements & Projects

Captaincy Milestones

Led India to victory in the 2007 ICC T20 World Cup, 2011 ICC ODI World Cup, and 2013 ICC Champions Trophy, becoming one of the most successful captains in cricket history.

Wicketkeeping Excellence

Renowned for quick reflexes and smart stumping, Dhoni revolutionized wicketkeeping with his agility and unique style behind the stumps.

Rising Pune Supergiant

Co-owned IPL team Rising Pune Supergiant, investing in talent development and sports management.

Philanthropy & Mentorship

Actively supports various charities and mentors young cricketers, fostering the next generation of sports talent in India.
